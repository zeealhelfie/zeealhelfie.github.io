Quickbase Architecture & Internal Tools

Role: Quickbase admin & solution designer · Stack: Quickbase, formulas, Pipelines, Excel, APIs

Most of my automations sit on top of Quickbase apps that I designed and maintain. I build the tables, relationships, forms, and formula fields that make it possible to drive documents, workflows, and reports directly from structured data.

Core Quickbase Design Work

I’ve designed and maintained several Quickbase applications that support:

  • Employee and HR data (for timesheets, pay cycles, and holidays).
  • Project and site structures for telecom work (T-Mobile, Verizon, etc.).
  • Fields mapped directly into Excel/Word templates and automation scripts.

This includes decisions about field types, relationships, lookup fields, summary fields, and how data should be surfaced on forms and reports.

Quickbase Integrations & Internal Tools

On top of the schema design, I build internal tools and reports that make Quickbase easier to use for non-technical team members:

  • Forms optimized for project managers, HR, and finance workflows.
  • Formula fields that calculate derived values (e.g., pay-period logic, status).
  • Reports that power dashboards and drive automation triggers.
  • Integration points that connect to Google Cloud Functions and Box.

See also: Quickbase Automation project page.

Inventory & Office Liquidation Tools

I also designed tools to track physical assets (chairs, desks, monitors, cabinets, etc.) and support office inventory and liquidation planning.

  • Structured Excel templates for capturing item details and conditions.
  • Quickbase fields for item type, location, value, and resale status.
  • Reports that group items by category for potential sale or reuse.

See also: Inventory project page.

Impact

  • Created a solid data foundation for multiple automations and reports.
  • Reduced the need for one-off spreadsheets by centralizing logic in Quickbase.
  • Made it easier for other team members to self-serve the data they need.
