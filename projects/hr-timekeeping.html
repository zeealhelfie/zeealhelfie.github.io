HR & Timekeeping Automations (Kronos + Quickbase)

Role: Internal tools & automation · Stack: Python, Google Cloud Functions, Kronos, Quickbase, Pipelines

I built a suite of automations around Kronos and Quickbase to keep timesheets, holidays, and out-of-office information accurate without manual data entry. These functions reduce repetitive work for HR and managers and make it easier to trust the data in our systems.

Context & Problem

HR and timekeeping workflows involve multiple systems: Kronos for time entry, Quickbase for tracking projects and employees, and internal processes for pay periods, holidays, and approvals. Originally, much of the synchronization was done manually:

  • Exporting data out of Kronos and importing into Quickbase
  • Manually adjusting for holidays and special pay rules
  • Copying out-of-office information into tracking sheets
  • Reconciling discrepancies across systems at the end of each pay period

This was time-consuming, error-prone, and difficult to scale as more employees and projects were added.

Key Automations

Kronos Weekly Timesheets Sync (kronos_timesheets)

A Google Cloud Function that runs on a schedule and updates weekly timesheet records in Quickbase using data from Kronos.

  • Pulls Kronos data for the current pay period or week
  • Maps employees and project codes to Quickbase records
  • Writes or updates time entry records in the EMPLOYEE_WEEKLY_TIMESHEETS table
  • Applies logic for weeks that run Saturday → Friday

Kronos Holiday Calendar Automation (kronos_holiday)

Automation that ensures holiday schedules and HR rules are consistently applied.

  • Identifies holiday dates and marks them correctly in Quickbase
  • Supports pay-period logic and avoids manual overrides
  • Makes downstream reporting and approvals more accurate

Employee Out-of-Office → HR Sync (kronos-outofoffice-email)

Weekly automation that reads out-of-office or off-work data and updates HR tracking.

  • Collects OOO / off-work information
  • Summarizes it into a structured format for HR
  • Updates Quickbase so managers have a centralized view

Kronos Connector Pipeline (kronos-connector-pipeline)

Glue layer between Kronos, Quickbase, and my cloud functions.

  • Handles triggering logic (per week or pay period)
  • Coordinates data flows into the right Quickbase tables
  • Reduces the number of manual touchpoints in the workflow

Architecture Overview

  • Kronos – source of time entries and schedule information.
  • Google Cloud Functions – Python services to transform and load data.
  • Quickbase – system-of-record for employees, projects, and timesheets.
  • Quickbase Pipelines / Webhooks – triggers and glue between systems.

Impact

  • Reduced manual HR data entry for weekly timesheets.
  • Improved consistency of pay-period and holiday handling.
  • Gave managers a more reliable view of who is available or out-of-office.
  • Created a foundation for future HR-related automation on top of Quickbase.
