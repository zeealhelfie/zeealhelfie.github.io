Finance & Procurement Automation (Ariba + Bulk Uploads)

Role: Internal tools & integrations · Stack: Python, Google Cloud, Ariba, Quickbase, Excel

I built a set of bulk upload tools and integrations that move purchase order and pricing data from Ariba into our finance and project systems. These automations replace manual spreadsheet work and make large PO datasets easier to manage and audit.

Problem

Finance and project teams receive large volumes of PO lines, quotes, and pricing tables from Ariba and other systems. Historically, this data was:

  • Exported as spreadsheets with inconsistent structures
  • Manually cleaned and re-formatted before upload
  • Copied into internal tools line-by-line
  • Difficult to reconcile when something changed in Ariba

The result was a lot of time spent massaging spreadsheets instead of managing projects and budgets.

Finance Bulk Upload & Ariba Integration Toolkit

I grouped several scripts and pipelines into a toolkit that standardizes finance imports:

  • verizon_ariba_import – pulls Verizon Ariba feeds into a structured format.
  • verizon_ariba_core_sync – keeps internal “core” data synced with Ariba changes.
  • PO_Line_Codes, POLineItems, pricing_table – normalize and bulk import PO and pricing data.
  • PurchaseOrder&ChangeOrderRequest – ingests purchase orders and change orders.
  • Quotes_and_POR, Vendor_PO_Line_Items – handle vendor-specific and quote-based inputs.

Each script focuses on one type of dataset, but they share common patterns for validation, error reporting, and mapping to the target schema.

New Award & PO Line Notifications

In addition to bulk upload tools, I implemented notifications for new awards and PO line changes.

  • Watches for new awards or PO lines in Ariba-related data.
  • Sends alerts so finance and project managers don’t miss important changes.
  • Helps the team react quickly when scope or funding shifts.

Architecture Overview

  • Ariba / vendor portals – source of PO, pricing, and award data.
  • Python & Google Cloud – scripts and functions that clean and transform this data.
  • Quickbase / internal systems – final destination for structured, validated records.
  • Bulk upload patterns – consistent mapping and validation logic across datasets.

Impact

  • Reduced manual spreadsheet cleanup for large PO datasets.
  • Improved reliability of financial data in internal systems.
  • Shorter turnaround time from award to usable project data.
  • Created reusable patterns for future finance-related automations.
